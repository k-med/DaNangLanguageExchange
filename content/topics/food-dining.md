Food is one of the easiest ways to connect across languages. This worksheet is designed for cafe tables in Da Nang, so members can start simple, then move into recommendations, stories, and culture.

<div class="social-proof-banner">
  <strong>Best for:</strong> first-time visitors, mixed-level tables, and any session where you want people talking quickly.
</div>

### Beginner Vocabulary

- **ngon**: delicious
- **ăn**: to eat
- **uống**: to drink
- **món ăn**: dish
- **quán ăn**: local eatery
- **gọi món**: to order food
- **cay**: spicy
- **ngọt**: sweet

### Beginner Questions

1. What is your favorite Vietnamese dish?
2. What did you eat today?
3. Do you usually cook at home or eat outside?
4. Do you prefer coffee, tea, or juice?
5. What food do you miss from your home country?

### Intermediate Questions

1. Which cafe in Da Nang do you recommend to a friend and why?
2. How is food culture in Vietnam different from your home country?
3. What dish did you try in Vietnam that surprised you?
4. Is street food better than restaurant food? Explain your answer.

### Advanced / Open Prompts

- How do food habits change when a city becomes more international?
- Does sharing food make conversation easier? Why?
- What does a “real local food experience” mean, and who gets to define it?

### Vietnamese Challenge

<div class="challenge-box vietnamese">
  Order your drink in Vietnamese, then ask your partner: <em>Ban thich mon nao nhat o Da Nang?</em> What dish do you like most in Da Nang?
</div>

### English Challenge

<div class="challenge-box english">
  Describe one favorite dish using at least three texture or taste words such as crispy, rich, sour, smoky, or refreshing.
</div>

### QR-Friendly Prompt Set

- Best cheap breakfast in Da Nang?
- One Vietnamese dish every visitor should try?
- A food you did not like at first but learned to enjoy?
- Cafe drink order in one sentence?
